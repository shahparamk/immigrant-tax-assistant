Core Features

Built for nonresident aliens

Every feature is designed around the specific needs of international students and workers navigating the U.S. tax system.

💬
Profile-aware chat
Answers tailored to your visa type, home country, and years in the US. F-1 and H-1B get different guidance because the rules genuinely differ.
📚
Citation-mandatory responses
Every answer cites specific IRS publications and page numbers. No generic advice — every fact is traceable to a source document.
⚠️
CPA escalation
24 complex topics (FBAR, crypto, state taxes, audits) are auto-detected and immediately redirected to licensed professionals.
📅
SPT calculator
Deterministic Substantial Presence Test — pure Python with no LLM involvement. Zero hallucination on the math that determines your tax status.
📋
Filing checklist
Generates a personalized tax filing checklist from your session — forms, documents, deadlines, and treaty benefits specific to your profile.
🌍
7-country treaty support
Country-specific treaty guidance for India, China, South Korea, Germany, Mexico, Canada, and Japan — including specific article references.
⚠️ This tool provides general tax information based on IRS publications (Tax Year 2024/2025). It is NOT a substitute for professional tax advice. Always consult a licensed CPA for complex situations.
System Architecture

Six-layer RAG pipeline

Every query flows through safety checks and profile-aware retrieval before the LLM is invoked — ensuring grounded, accurate answers every time.

01
User query
Visa type + home country + question via Gradio web interface
02
CPA escalation check
24 keywords detected → immediate CPA referral, no LLM call made
03
Metadata filter
Country + visa type narrow the candidate pool from 3,726 chunks
04
FAISS semantic search
384-dim cosine similarity via all-MiniLM-L6-v2 · fetches top 15
05
Smart re-ranking
9 signal categories apply 1.1x–1.6x boosts to surface correct sources
06
Prompt engineering
Profile + top-5 IRS chunks + citation-mandatory system prompt
07
Groq LLaMA 3.1 8B Instant
temperature=0.0 → cited answer + IRS sources
